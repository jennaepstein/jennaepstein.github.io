Geospatial Risk Modeling: Predicting Thefts from Automobiles in Washington, DC


For this project, I built a geospatial risk model optimizing for accuracy and generalizability to predict incidents of theft from automobiles in Washington, DC. Acknowledging the challenges of selection bias and clear critiques around predictive policing, this project leverages an open-source framework to develop risk predictions and compare them to more traditional approaches around hotspot mapping.
