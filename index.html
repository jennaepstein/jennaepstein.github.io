Predicting Wildfire Risk and Preparing Communities in Southern California

For this project, Jeff Stern and I developed a wildfire risk prediction model and interactive application for evacuation kit distribution in Southern California. Incorporating environmental and spatial risk factors, our linear model outputs probabilities of wildfire occurrence to represent risk. Our interactive application combines the Census tract-level wildfire risk with CDC's Social Vulnerability Index in a final algorithm.

View Project Markdown | Explore Application

Software & Tools: R, RShiny, ArcGIS

Predicting Incidents of Theft from Automobiles in Washington, DC

For this project, I built a geospatial risk model optimizing for accuracy and generalizability to predict incidents of theft from automobiles in DC. Acknowledging the challenges of selection bias and clear critiques around predictive policing, this project leverages an open-source framework to develop risk predictions and compare them to more traditional approaches around hotspot mapping.
